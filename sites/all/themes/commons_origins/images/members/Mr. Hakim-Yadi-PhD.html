Vice President

Dr Hakim Yadi is a strategy consultant specializing in the fields of technology development and translational medicine.

Hakim provides precious guidance in the high level strategy of the GHN. Hakim is an advocate of integrating clinical insight, academic research and industry know-how. This passion in translational science led him from academia and industry to work for a translational medicine consultancy company based in Cambridge UK called 4D Biomedical. Whilst at 4D, Hakim worked on supporting research institutes and hospitals create the right environment, management and infrastructure to support the collaboration between industry, academia and clinicians.

Hakim worked on the formation of the Institute for Cardiovascular Science (ICS) at UCL that brought together the Heart Hospital, Great Ormond Street and University London College Hospital. Working on the ICS involved coordinating cardiologists across multiple sites and from different academic backgrounds. Hakim has also worked on creating these translational science environments in gastroenterology and orthopedics.

Hakim has also structured a joint venture between a large NHS trust and a leading UK university and has worked on the formation of a biomarker and diagnostics start-up company based on University owned intellectual property. Dr Yadi holds a first class honors degree in Biology from the University of Bath and a PhD in Molecular Biology from Cambridge University and is the author of several scientific papers Many people stated their interest and volunteered their time to participate on the steering committee, which has led to a diverse group of members.
